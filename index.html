How To Make Phamtastic Pizza

Tony Pham

Wednesday, October 28, 2020

A Pizza Made By An Aspiring Web Developer

There are numerous ways to go about making pizza but out of the ones I have seen, I cannot say I have run into one written by someone who wants to be a web developer. It makes sense given in general, tutorials are given by people who feel that they know enough to teach others on their craft and I cannot say I have seen a pizza making tutorial among a playlist of web development courses. While I am definitely not a professional baker, two things I really enjoy in life are web development and pizza so I figured why not combine the two using this webpage. Here I share my method for making pizza and as you will see, it is likely very different from other tutorials available online.

Some Of The Things You Will Need

Below you will find some of the items you will need to make a Phamtastic Pizza.

Cooking Materials

A snapshot of some of the cooking materials you will need is provided in Figure 1.

  • Kettle
  • Stove (or a cooktop for boiling water with the kettle)
  • Small rice bowl
  • Small plate
  • Teaspoons
  • Soup spoon
  • Large bowl
  • Chopsticks (yes, you will need chopsticks!)
  • Cutting board (large enough to fit your pizza)
  • Cloth (large enough to cover the large bowl)
  • Aluminum foil
  • Scissors
  • Knife
  • Pizza roller
  • Cheese grater
  • Toaster oven (or just use your regular oven if you have one)
  • Pizza cutter

Ingredients

A snapshot of some of the ingredients you will need is provided in Figure 2.

  • Yeast
  • Warm Water (defined as you can dip your finger in it and not get burned)
  • Sugar
  • All-purpose flour
  • Olive Oil (vegetable oil works too but I like olive oil better)
  • Salt
  • Pasta Sauce (I like Prego, but you can of course use any sauce you would like)
  • A block of cheese
  • Toppings

Preparing The Yeast

This will take approximately 30 minutes.

Boil some tap water in a kettle over a stove set on high. Once the water has come to a boil, pour it into a small rice bowl about 1/4 of the way full as shown in Figure 3.

Leave the water to cool down for about 8 minutes. Add one teaspoon of sugar into the small rice bowl and stir.

The sugar will act as food for the yeast.

Wait for 1 minute and then add 1 ½ teaspoons of yeast into the small rice bowl. Stir the small rice bowl until the yeast dissolves as shown in Figure 4.

It is normal to observe bubbles and some of the yeast clustering together.

Cover the small rice bowl with a small plate and wait between 10 and 15 minutes. Remove the small plate from the top of the small rice bowl and you should notice the yeast has risen as shown in Figure 5.

Making The Dough

This step should take approximately 10 minutes to complete.

Add 6 soup spoon's worth of All-purpose flour into a large bowl. Next, add 1 tea spoon of salt. Stir the contents using a pair of chopsticks.

Do not add salt directly to the small rice bowl containing your yeast-sugar-water mixture as this would kill the yeast.

Pour the contents of the small rice bowl from the previous section into the large bowl. Add warm water to the small rice bowl until it is full. Stir the warm water in the small rice bowl to collect the yeast which has clung to the bowl. Pour the contents into the large bowl. Add one tea spoon of olive oil into the large bowl. Mix the large bowl contents using a pair of chopsticks until the dough begins to form.

If the dough seems to be really wet and does not form, more soup spoons of all-purpose flour one spoonful at a time until the dough forms.

Kneading And The Dough Bounces Back

This step should take approximately 15 minutes to complete.

Add a soup spoonful of all-purpose flour onto the cutting board and spread it out. Remove the dough from the large bowl and put the dough onto the cutting board. Knead the dough on the cutting board. To knead the dough, what you are effectively doing is folding the dough on itself and pushing on it repeatedly. Below you will find a short video which demonstrates this process.

In general you will want to knead the dough for 10 minutes but a better indicator of when to stop is by poking the dough. When you poke the dough, if it bounces back then you know you have kneaded enough.

Once you have finished kneading the dough add one tea spoon of olive oil into the large bowl. Using your finger, spread the olive oil evenly throughout the bowl.

The olive oil keeps the dough from sticking to the bowl.

Place the dough into the large bowl. Cover the bowl with a large cloth.
